About the different quality savings of my video, the low was highly degraded in quality. Even if the size of the file was very low (6.03MB) I would never use it on a website. I could see moving the pixels as the video was playing and the images were changing. The medium quality produced an optimal video (60.08MB). It was good to watch with clear output and would be acceptable to post on a website. High quality had even better visual quality, but the size of the file is much bigger as well (180MB). In this case, it is still alright as the video itself is less than two minutes but it definitely needs to be considered in vaster video elements to not affect the user experience badly.
