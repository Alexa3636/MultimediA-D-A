Defining your Audience

Tasks 1 - Identifying your audience(s)

  • Age:
    • The primary target audience will be teenagers, adults, and older groups who can recognize the issue
    and do the appropriate research on it.However, in some way, the younger audience needs to be considered
    as a passive audience because there is a possibility that parents will do the research and, therefore,
    come across the website to find solutions for their children's problematic sleep.
  • Gender:
    • Not specified.
  • Language proficiency:
    • English
  • Educational level:
    • Basic literacy of computer use, besides reading and decoding skills, will be necessary to navigate the
    website and find it. Therefore, potential users do not need any specific educational background.
    The website will provide links to scientific research data for users with academic backgrounds; however,
    the used academic publications and references will be summarised and “translated” into an easily digestible
    form for the primary users on the main website.
  • Attitude towards writer or organisation:
    • Several academic research studies examined the idea of the different digital audio formats, such as audiobooks,
    podcasts, or music (meditative), their positive effects on sleeping quality, and their use as a possible alternative
    solution for insomnia. However, as with every alternative solution for a significant issue, the opinions differ in
    their effectiveness.
  • Knowledge of the topic:
    • o Already using it as an alternative solution for insomnia – recurring browser
    o Sceptic about it – never tried it but heard about it / trust in medication.
    o Information seeker - Still in the dark – Does not have a name for the life-changing issue or just got introduced to the aspect of insomnia.
  • Audience action:
    • The website will focus on three digital audio types, audiobooks, podcasts and music, as an alternative solution for a
    common mental health issue: insomnia. The preferable outcome is that through interaction and the provided information,
    users will find an understanding of their mental health issue (insomnia) and preferably find an efficient alternative solution
    for this problem by introducing them to the benefits of the mentioned digital audio types and providing ways to try them out.
    Also, the website will provide interaction among different users in some way (e.g., forum) to enhance information flow and
    create opportunities to present feedback or new ideas for each other from first-hand experiences.

Task 3 - Exploring the audience needs in relation to design

The overall design will focus on creating a peaceful and harmonious interface for the users in consideration of the website's main purpose,
which is to provide understanding and preferably some relief for them about their or their loved one's mental health issue. For instance,
it will be reflected through the offered alternatives (audiobook, boadcast, music) that will be presented to invite the users to try them out
in order to find the most efficient one for their needs. Also, the proposed forum function of the website could contribute to creating a converging community
that enhances the website's content and supports individuals to open towards the community. The design and the chosen colour scheme (night sky) will create
a calming interface associated with the topic. The presented multimedia elements will be varied to inform and interact with the users and
also provide emotional connections and responses. Creating a straightforward navigation system will also be focused on, considering the broad target audience
and the contingent state of some potential users (lack of sleep – hardship in focusing and slow responses).
